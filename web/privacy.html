Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 1, 2026

Overview

StumbleClone (“the Game”) is a casual party game. We respect your privacy and keep data collection to a minimum. This policy explains what the Game stores and how it is used.

Information the Game stores

  • Display name — if you enter a player name, it is stored only on your device to label your scores on the local leaderboard. It is not sent to us or any server.
  • Local game data — high scores, settings, and your selected character skin are saved locally on your device (browser storage on web; app preferences on mobile).

What we do NOT do

  • We do not require an account or login.
  • We do not collect your real name, email, precise location, or contacts.
  • We do not sell or share personal data with third parties.
  • We do not currently use third-party advertising or analytics SDKs. If this changes, this policy will be updated and, where required, you will be asked for consent.

Where data is stored

All game data is stored locally on your device — browser localStorage on the web, and app preferences on iOS / Android. Clearing your browser data or uninstalling the app removes it.

Hosting and platform data

The web version is hosted on Vercel, which may collect standard server logs (such as IP address and browser type) as part of normal, secure web hosting. When you install the Game from the App Store or Google Play, those platforms may collect data under their own policies (for example, crash reports or store analytics). Please review Vercel, Apple, and Google policies for details.

Children’s privacy

The Game is suitable for general audiences. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Because no personal data leaves your device, no such information is collected by us.

Your choices

You can remove all locally stored game data at any time by clearing your browser storage (web) or uninstalling the app (mobile).

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the Game evolves (for example, if an online leaderboard is added). The “Last updated” date above reflects the most recent version.
